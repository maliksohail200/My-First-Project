Dr. Israr Ahmed It’ll blow your mind.

Dr. Israr Ahmed (1932–2010) was a renowned Islamic scholar, philosopher, and preacher from Pakistan, widely respected for his deep knowledge of the Qur'an and commitment to Islamic revival. Born in Hisar, India, he migrated to Pakistan after the partition in 1947. Initially pursuing a career in medicine, he earned his MBBS degree from King Edward Medical College, Lahore, but soon shifted his focus to Islamic education and activism. Early in his life, he was associated with Jamaat-e-Islami but later founded his own movement, Tanzeem-e-Islami, in 1975, aiming to establish the Islamic system through peaceful efforts. Dr. Israr devoted his life to teaching the Qur'an, offering detailed explanations (Tafsir) through public lectures, radio, and television. His famous Qur'anic lectures earned him immense popularity not only in Pakistan but also among Urdu-speaking communities worldwide. Despite his strong political views, he always emphasized non-violent methods for bringing about Islamic change. Throughout his life, he authored several books on Islamic philosophy, the Qur'an, and the challenges facing the Muslim world. Dr. Israr Ahmed passed away on April 14, 2010, in Lahore, leaving behind a legacy of intellectual and spiritual awakening for Muslims.

Maulana Tariq Jamil It’ll blow your mind.

Maulana Tariq Jamil is a renowned Pakistani Islamic scholar, preacher, and public speaker, known for his inspiring sermons promoting peace, love, and unity among Muslims. Born in 1953 in Tulamba, Punjab, he initially pursued medical studies at King Edward Medical College but later dedicated his life to Islamic learning and preaching. He received his religious education from Jamia Arabia, Raiwind. Associated with the Tableeghi Jamaat, Maulana Tariq Jamil traveled extensively across the world, delivering simple, heartfelt messages that emphasize character building, truthfulness, and obedience to Allah. His speeches often bridge the gap between modern society and Islamic values. Widely respected across all sections of society, he continues to play a major role in promoting a positive image of Islam globally.

Ajmal Raza QadriIt’ll blow your mind.

Peer Ajmal Raza Qadri is a well-known Pakistani Islamic scholar, spiritual leader, and naat khwan (reciter of Islamic poetry), associated with the Barelvi school of thought. Born in Pakistan, he dedicated his early years to Islamic education, studying classical religious sciences such as Qur'an, Hadith, Fiqh (Islamic law), and Tasawwuf (spirituality). Known for his powerful speeches filled with love for the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ), Peer Ajmal Raza Qadri has been a passionate advocate for promoting the teachings of Ahle Sunnat Wal Jamaat. His bayanat (religious speeches) emphasize love for the Prophet, respect for the companions (Sahaba) and saints (Auliya), and following Shariah. His style of speech is emotional yet scholarly, touching the hearts of listeners across Pakistan and among Urdu-speaking communities worldwide. Over the years, Peer Ajmal Raza Qadri has traveled extensively, addressing large gatherings at Milad-un-Nabi events, Urs Mubarak celebrations, and religious conferences. Through his efforts, he has inspired countless people to reconnect with Islamic traditions, strengthen their faith, and develop deeper love for the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ). Today, he continues to be an influential figure among Sunni Muslims, respected for his devotion, simplicity, and sincere service to Islam. His naats and manqabats are widely listened to and loved for their emotional depth and religious fervor. Peer Ajmal Raza Qadri is often invited as a chief guest in major religious events across Pakistan and overseas, where his presence draws large crowds.
